General Information - Pula

About Pula

Pula is a seafront city on the southern tip of Croatia's Istrian Peninsula, known for its protected harbor, beach-lined coast, and Roman ruins.

The city has a long tradition as a popular summer vacation destination, with many fascinating cultural attractions and historic monuments.

Weather

July Weather in Pula:

  • Average high temperature: 29°C (84°F)
  • Average low temperature: 18°C (64°F)
  • Water temperature: 24°C (75°F)
  • Sunshine: 10-11 hours per day
  • Rainfall: Low (typically 2-3 days with some rain in the month)

It is recommended to bring light summer clothes, sunscreen, sunglasses, and perhaps a light jacket for evening outings.

Transportation

Local Transportation

Pula has a reliable public bus service that connects most parts of the city. Single tickets cost approximately 2€.

Taxi Services: Uber and Bolt are available in Pula, along with traditional taxi services.

Getting to Pula

  • By Air: Pula Airport (PUY) is located about 6 km from the city center.
  • By Bus: Pula is well-connected to other Croatian cities and neighboring countries.
  • By Car: The Istrian Y highway connects Pula with the rest of Croatia.
