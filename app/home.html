Invited Lecture 1: Educational Robotics
Assoc. Prof. Ana Sović Kržić
Educational Robotics: Transforming Robots into Educational Tools

Ana Sović Kržić from the University of Zagreb explores how robots can support learning in science and engineering. She talks about different types of educational robots, how teachers feel about using them in the classroom, and what students think about robots through a storytelling activity. At the end of the lecture, she will present Robi, the first humanoid robot built in Croatia.

Invited Lecture 2: Additive Manufacturing
Full Prof. Damir Godec
Quo vadis Additive Manufacturing?

Damir Godec from the Faculty of Mechanical Engineering and Naval Architecture in Zagreb presents current trends in additive manufacturing. The lecture also looks at how artificial intelligence is helping shape the future of this technology.

Invited Lecture 3: Interfacing the Invisible
Martine-Nicole Rojina
Interfacing the Invisible: Inclusive Sensory Invitations Across Cultures, Species, and Artificial Agents

Martine-Nicole Rojina is an artist and educator who focuses on creating new ways of connecting across cultures, species and technologies. In this talk, she shares her experiences from working in remote and extreme environments and shows how perception and emotion can be used to design more inclusive and meaningful interactions. Her work combines sound, light, movement and storytelling to explore how we relate to each other and to the world around us.

Moderator: Damir Medved

Panelists: Hochrinner, Hagen Helge, Gordana Nikolić, Martina Hervat and Biljana Martinčić

We'll delve into how this integrated approach, combining classroom learning with practical, on-the-job training, benefits both individuals and industries. Experts will share insights on successful models, challenges, and future prospects for dual education, highlighting its significance in fostering skilled talent and bridging the gap between academia and real-world demands. Join us to discuss strategies for strengthening vocational pathways and enhancing employability.

What to See in Pula & Around

Make the most of your visit to Pula! Here are the top attractions and activities to explore during your stay.

Historical Attractions in Pula

Pula Arena

Roman amphitheatre from the 1st century - one of the best-preserved Roman amphitheatres in the world.

Must-see attraction, hosts concerts and events

Temple of Augustus & Forum

Ancient Roman square in the old town center with well-preserved Roman temple.

Perfect for photos and historical exploration

Zerostrasse Tunnels

WWI underground shelters beneath the city - a unique underground tour experience.

Cool refuge from summer heat

Kaštel & Historical Museum

Hilltop fortress with panoramic views of Pula and the Adriatic Sea.

Best views in the city, great for sunset

Waterfront & Nature

Riva & Lungomare Promenade

Ideal for an evening stroll by the sea. The waterfront promenade offers beautiful views, cafes, and restaurants.

Perfect for networking walks and relaxation

Accommodation Tips

Peak Season Notice: July is high tourist season in Pula. Book accommodation well in advance!

  • Hotels near city center: Hotel Scaletta, Hotel Omir
  • Budget options: Youth Hostel Pula, private apartments
  • Luxury: Park Plaza Histria, Hotel Brioni

Getting Around Pula

  • Walking: City center is compact and walkable
  • Public transport: City buses connect main areas
  • Taxi: Available, but more expensive during tourist season
  • Bike rental: Several rental shops in the city center
